Solar System

The solar system consists of the Sun, eight planets, moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets, all bound by the Sun's gravity. At its center is the Sun, a massive star providing energy through nuclear fusion. The planets are divided into rocky terrestrial planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) and gas giants (Jupiter, Saturn) and ice giants (Uranus, Neptune). Earth is the only planet known to support life. Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt, home to dwarf planets like Pluto. The solar system also includes countless smaller bodies like meteoroids and interstellar dust, forming a dynamic system that orbits the Milky Way.
